1 Executive Summary

Key Strength

Premium formulations with clinically-dosed, patented ingredients (enfinity, PEAK ATP, Synapsa) differentiate States from commodity supplements.

Core Opportunity

"Choose Your State" positioning is unique in the market. The on-demand, experiential nature of the products is underutilized in current messaging.

Primary Gap

Time-of-day usage guidance is inconsistent. Users need clearer protocols for when to take each State and how to stack them.

Key Findings

  1. Paraxanthine (enfinity) is undermarketed - This is the most innovative ingredient across the line. The "Coffee 2.0" messaging from Vishen's videos should be more prominent on the website.
  2. State stacking creates upsell opportunities - Email campaigns mention combining BLISS+GAZM and FOCUS+ZEN, but the website doesn't educate users on stacking protocols.
  3. Scientific credibility is strong but buried - 23+ clinical studies support the ingredients, but this evidence isn't prominently displayed on product pages.
  4. The Quiz is too short - The current 3-question quiz doesn't capture enough information to provide truly personalized recommendations including time-of-day guidance.
  5. Pricing psychology favors the Box Set - At $299 for all 5 States (normally $395+), the Box Set is the obvious value choice. Individual product pages should reinforce this.

Shawn Wells frames supplements as "high vibration" solutions for optimizing natural brain function, contrasting them with pharmaceuticals/drugs which are "low vibration" because they create dependency and artificial states. This resonates with Mindvalley's conscious, personal-growth audience.

  1. "Design Your Mood on Demand" - Vishen's core promise. Currently buried.

  3. "Experiential Supplements" - Unlike vitamins you can't feel, States create a noticeable shift within 20-30 minutes.
  4. "Sovereignty Over Your State" - Personal autonomy messaging appeals to the self-empowerment audience.
  5. "The Anti-Drug Approach" - Working with your brain's natural chemistry, not against it.

4 Website Optimization

Priority Improvements

1

Upgrade the State Assessment Quiz to 10 Questions

Current 3-question quiz is too shallow. A 10-question quiz can capture lifestyle factors, time-of-day needs, and provide personalized stack recommendations with usage protocols.

2

Add "Coffee 2.0" Section to Homepage

Paraxanthine is the most innovative ingredient and appeals to the massive coffee-drinking market. Create a dedicated section explaining why it's the "next evolution of caffeine."

3

Create State Stacking Guide Page

A dedicated page showing recommended combinations: Presentation Stack, Social Stack, Recovery Stack, etc. This increases average order value and improves user outcomes.

4

Add Time-of-Day Icons to Product Cards

Simple visual cues (morning sun, afternoon, evening moon, night stars) help users quickly understand when to use each State.

5

Expand Science Page with Clinical Study Summaries

Create expandable sections for each ingredient showing: study name, participants, results, and citation. This builds trust with skeptical buyers.

Product Page Enhancements

Each individual product page should include:

  • Mechanism of Action section - Simple explanation of how it works in the brain
  • Best Time to Take - Clear guidance with clock icon
  • Pairs Well With - Cross-sell complementary States
  • What You'll Feel - Set expectations for the experience
  • Clinical Evidence accordion - Expandable section with study summaries
  • Box Set CTA - "Get all 5 States for just $99" reminder on every page

Homepage Improvements

  • Add "Daily State Protocol" visual showing which State to use at each time of day
  • Add video testimonials from real users (more compelling than text)
  • Feature the "Coffee 2.0" angle prominently in hero or first section
  • Add Shawn Wells credentials more prominently - his track record is a major differentiator
  • Include a "What Makes Us Different" comparison section

Day 1: Welcome + How to take your first State. Day 3: What to expect. Day 7: Check-in + stacking tips. Day 14: Request review.

State-Specific Education

If someone buys FOCUS only, send them content about FOCUS benefits, then introduce complementary States (ZEN for calm focus, DREAM for recovery).

Reorder Reminder (Day 25)

Based on 30-day supply, remind customers to reorder before they run out. Include a small incentive for subscription sign-up.

Win-Back for Single-State Buyers

30 days after purchase, offer Box Set at special price to customers who only bought one State. "Now that you've experienced FOCUS, try the complete system."
