AEO FAQ Optimization Guide

How to write FAQs that get picked up by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews — and reduce customer support tickets

1 What is AEO?

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)

The practice of structuring content so AI systems (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Siri, Alexa) can easily extract and cite your answers as authoritative sources.

Traditional SEO optimizes for Google's blue links. AEO optimizes for AI-generated answers that increasingly replace those links.

Why This Matters for Mindvalley

  • ✓ Discovery: When someone asks "what are the best nootropics for focus", we want our content cited
  • ✓ Trust: AI-sourced answers carry implicit credibility ("According to Mindvalley...")
  • ✓ Support Deflection: Well-structured FAQs answer questions before they become tickets
  • ✓ Pre-Purchase Education: Informed customers convert better and return less

2 The Problem with Traditional FAQs

Most FAQs are written from a brand-centric perspective. They answer questions about the product, not questions people actually search for.

❌ Brand-Centric (Old Way)

"Will FOCUS make me jittery like coffee?"

✓ Search-Intent (AEO Way)

"Do nootropics cause jitters like caffeine?"

❌ Brand-Centric (Old Way)

"How do I use Mindvalley Meditation?"

✓ Search-Intent (AEO Way)

"How long should I meditate as a beginner?"

❌ Brand-Centric (Old Way)

"What's included in The Silva Method Quest?"

✓ Search-Intent (AEO Way)

"What is the Silva Method and does it work?"

The key insight: Brand-specific questions only match branded searches. Generic questions capture discovery traffic — people who don't know about you yet.

3 The AEO Answer Formula

AI systems extract the first 1-2 sentences for snippets. If your key answer is buried in paragraph 3, it won't get surfaced. Use the Inverted Pyramid:

// The AEO Answer Structure

[1] DIRECT ANSWER — Bold, definitive, 1 sentence
[2] EVIDENCE — Stats, studies, clinical data
[3] MECHANISM — How/why it works
[4] BRAND TIE-IN — How your product delivers this
[5] CTA — Optional, subtle

Example Transformation

❌ Before (Answer Buried)

"Most people notice effects within 20-30 minutes. FOCUS and ZEN tend to kick in fastest (20-30 min), BLISS works within 30-45 minutes. The activation time can vary based on individual biochemistry, whether you've eaten, and your current state."

↓

✓ After (Answer First)

"Most nootropics take 20-45 minutes to feel effects, though some build up over weeks. Fast-acting compounds: Paraxanthine and L-Theanine work within 20-30 minutes. Medium-acting: Adaptogens like Ashwagandha take 30-45 minutes. Cumulative compounds: Bacopa (Synapsa®) takes 2-4 weeks of daily use to show full cognitive benefits, as it works by promoting neuroplasticity."

Notice: The rewritten version leads with a definitive statement that AI can extract, then provides supporting evidence.

4 Question Rewrite Formulas

Use these templates to convert brand-centric questions to search-intent questions:

// Formula 1: Product Concern → Category Concern
Brand: "Does [PRODUCT] cause [CONCERN]?"
AEO: "Does [INGREDIENT/CATEGORY] cause [CONCERN]?"

// Formula 2: Product How-To → Category How-To
Brand: "How do I use [PRODUCT]?"
AEO: "What's the best way to [ACHIEVE OUTCOME]?"

// Formula 3: Product Feature → User Need
Brand: "What's included in [PRODUCT]?"
AEO: "What do I need to [ACHIEVE GOAL]?"

// Formula 4: Brand Comparison → Category Comparison
Brand: "How is [PRODUCT] different from [COMPETITOR]?"
AEO: "What's the difference between [METHOD A] and [METHOD B]?"

5 Adding Schema.org Markup

Structured data tells Google, ChatGPT, and Perplexity exactly what's a question and what's an answer — no guessing needed.

Step 1: Add JSON-LD to the <head>

<!-- Add this inside <head> --> <script type="application/ld+json"> { "@context": "https://schema.org", "@type": "FAQPage", "mainEntity": [ { "@type": "Question", "name": "What is the Silva Method and does it work?", "acceptedAnswer": { "@type": "Answer", "text": "The Silva Method is a meditation and mental training program developed in the 1960s. Research shows it can improve focus, reduce stress, and enhance learning. A University of Trinity study found participants showed significant improvement in memory and concentration after 3 weeks." } }, { "@type": "Question", "name": "Your second question here...", "acceptedAnswer": { "@type": "Answer", "text": "Your answer here..." } } ] } </script>

Step 2: Add Microdata to FAQ HTML

<!-- Wrap each FAQ item with microdata --> <div class="faq-item" itemscope itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question"> <div class="faq-question" itemprop="name"> What is the Silva Method and does it work? </div> <div class="faq-answer" itemscope itemprop="acceptedAnswer" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"> <span itemprop="text"> <strong>The Silva Method is a meditation and mental training program developed in the 1960s.</strong> Research shows it can improve focus... </span> </div> </div>

Step 3: Validate Your Markup

  • ✓ Test JSON-LD at: validator.schema.org
  • ✓ Test rich results at: Google Rich Results Test
  • ✓ Ensure answer text has no broken HTML

7 Implementation Process

1

Audit Current FAQs (1-2 hours)

Export all current questions. Flag which are brand-specific vs. search-intent. Identify gaps by reviewing support tickets from the last 90 days.

2

Research Search Intent (1 hour)

Use AnswerThePublic, AlsoAsked, or Google's "People Also Ask" to find what people actually search for in your Quest's category.

3

Rewrite Questions & Answers (2-4 hours)

Apply the formulas above. Ensure every answer starts with a bold, direct statement. Add evidence and stats where possible.

4

Add Schema Markup (30 minutes)

Add JSON-LD to the page head. Add microdata attributes to FAQ HTML elements. Validate with Google's tools.

5

Measure & Iterate (Ongoing)

Track FAQ rich result impressions in Search Console. Monitor support ticket volume by category. Update monthly based on new questions.

✓ Quick Reference Checklist

Use this checklist when creating or reviewing FAQs:

  • □ Questions match search intent, not just brand queries
  • □ Every answer starts with bold, direct statement
  • □ Answers include evidence (stats, studies, numbers)
  • □ JSON-LD FAQPage schema in <head>
  • □ Microdata attributes on FAQ HTML elements
  • □ Schema validated with Google's tools
  • □ Categories cover: Method, Results, Beginners, Comparisons, Practical, Support
  • □ Top support ticket questions are addressed
  • □ Meta description includes key FAQ topics
